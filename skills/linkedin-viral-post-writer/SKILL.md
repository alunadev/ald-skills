# LinkedIn Viral Post Writer

This skill implements a proven system for creating high-performing LinkedIn content based on analysis of posts that have generated millions of impressions and driven substantial follower growth.

## Core Philosophy

**Quality over quantity**: One exceptional post outweighs five mediocre ones. In the era of AI-generated content, top 1% craftsmanship is the differentiator.

**Four essential elements for viral posts**:
1. Amazing hook
2. Top 1% craftsmanship  
3. Amazing value (information, emotion, or both)
4. Proven topic

## Hook Creation System

### Hook Structure Rules

**Character limits**:
- First two lines: 62 characters each
- Third line: 50 characters
- Use all three lines when possible (most creators only use one or two)

### Proven Hook Templates

**High-performance formats**:
- "If I had to learn [X] again, I would start here"
- "[Dollar amount] thing free" re-hook
- "Meet [description]: [impressive achievement]"
- Story-driven openings with concrete details
- Combining multiple templates for unique hooks

### Hook Development Process

1. **Build a swipe file**: Save and review great hooks daily
2. **Generate variations**: Create 10-20 alternative hooks before finalizing
3. **Test uniqueness**: Ensure the hook hasn't been used before by combining templates creatively
4. **Preview tool**: Visualize how the hook renders (consider AuthoredUp or similar)

### Anti-patterns to avoid
- Generic 7-word hooks (go longer if the story demands it)
- Starting with overused phrases
- Hooks that don't create curiosity or emotion

## Craftsmanship Standards

### Time investment principle
Top 1% craftsmanship typically requires spending top 1% time. Expect:
- 8-10+ hours for major posts
- Multiple drafts and iterations
- Deep research (not surface-level)
- Every character and pixel optimized

### Multi-step creation process
For premium content:
1. Ideation phase
2. Research and writing
3. Visual/graphic development (if applicable)
4. Multiple approval rounds
5. Final polish

### Quality indicators
- Content other creators would share to their audience
- Original insights, not regurgitated information
- Specific, concrete details over high-level generalities
- No "AI slop" or satisficing

## Value Creation Framework

### Information value
- Deep research (8+ hours for major topics)
- Concrete, specific details (not abstractions)
- Original insights or synthesis
- Stories with actual quotes and numbers
- Case studies with real data

### Emotional value
Consider multiple audience segments:
- What emotions does this evoke?
- Who feels represented?
- What feelings drive sharing behavior?
- Does it inspire, validate, or create kinship?

### Combined approach
The highest-performing posts integrate BOTH information and emotion:
- Tell stories with data
- Include human elements in technical content
- Use specific examples that resonate emotionally
- Balance teaching with inspiring

## Topic Selection Strategy

### Pre-validated content signals
- Topics others have gone viral with
- Current trending subjects (but find unique angles)
- Cross-platform validation (worked on X/Twitter, etc.)
- Subject matter people are actively discussing

### Validation methods
- Check if thought leaders posted on topic
- Search for existing viral threads
- Identify emerging trends early
- Test topic relevance to target audience

### Risk management
Even with great hooks, craftsmanship, and value, wrong topics fail. Always validate the topic has audience interest before investing significant time.

## Post Format Considerations

### Format hierarchy (by typical performance)
1. Carousels (highest engagement)
2. Infographics
3. Video posts
4. Image posts
5. Text posts (lowest, but still effective)

### Format selection criteria
- **Your preference**: Choose formats you enjoy creating consistently
- **Niche alignment**: Text works for VC content, video for lifestyle, selfies for LinkedIn growth
- **Sustainability**: Pick formats you can maintain long-term

### Breaking format rules
Great posts can succeed with any format if other elements are exceptional. Don't let format constraints limit creativity.

## Content Funnel Strategy

### Three content types

**Top of funnel (ToF)**:
- Broadly useful to large audiences
- Grows reach and followers
- Topics with universal appeal
- Shareable content

**Middle of funnel (MoF)**:
- Builds authority in your niche
- Demonstrates expertise
- More specialized topics
- Establishes credibility

**Bottom of funnel (BoF)**:
- Drives monetization
- Promotes products/services
- Direct CTAs
- Conversion-focused

### Ratio recommendations
- **High growth phase**: 70% ToF, 20% MoF, 10% BoF
- **Authority building**: 40% ToF, 40% MoF, 20% BoF
- **Monetization focus**: 30% ToF, 30% MoF, 40% BoF

### Balance warning
Too much BoF content = algorithm penalty and lower engagement
Too much ToF content = missed monetization opportunities
Always maintain a mix of all three types.

## Writing Process

### Research phase
1. Search for existing content on topic
2. Find 3-5 authoritative sources
3. Look for unique angles not covered
4. Collect concrete details, quotes, numbers
5. Identify emotional hooks

### Drafting phase
1. Write 10-20 hook options
2. Select top 3 hooks to test
3. Draft full post with hook #1
4. Focus on specific details over abstractions
5. Include concrete examples and data
6. Weave in emotional elements

### Refinement phase
1. Cut unnecessary words ruthlessly
2. Ensure every sentence adds value
3. Check for "AI slop" indicators
4. Verify all facts and numbers
5. Test hook rendering (preview tool)
6. Get feedback if possible

### AI assistance approach
- Use AI for hook brainstorming (generate 100+ options)
- Leverage AI for rewrites and polish
- Use cutting-edge models for best results
- Never accept first draft from AI
- Maintain human judgment and creativity

## Citation and Attribution Rules

**CRITICAL**: Always credit sources properly:
- Include "Credit:" with active @ mentions when using others' ideas
- If not in post body, credit in first comment
- Never pass off others' work as your own
- When using pre-validated content, attribution is mandatory
- Building reputation depends on ethical practices

## Posting Frequency Guidelines

### Frequency myths debunked
- Daily posting NOT required
- Quality beats frequency consistently
- High performers often post 3-4x per week
- More frequent posting = lower average engagement

### Recommended frequencies
- **Passive brand building**: 1x per month
- **Active but busy**: 1x per week  
- **Standard growth**: 3-5x per week
- **Aggressive growth**: 5-7x per week
- **Maximum**: 10-11x per week (with funnel balance)

### Consistency > frequency
Choose a sustainable cadence and maintain it. Sporadic posting harms growth more than lower frequency.

## Growth Myths Addressed

### Myth: Algorithm is harder now
**Reality**: Algorithm hasn't changed for growth. More creators = more competition. Solution: Create top 1% content.

### Myth: Need selfies, no links, no @mentions
**Reality**: These "rules" are flexible. Focus on the four essential elements instead. Great posts succeed regardless of format choices.

### Myth: Must post carousels/infographics
**Reality**: Format matters less than execution. Success is possible with any format if other elements are strong.

## Creating Your Market of One

### Unique positioning
Rather than competing in crowded niches, create your own category:
- Combine multiple interests/expertise areas
- Share unique personal experiences
- Develop signature content types
- Make yourself un-copyable through authentic stories

### Format: "The [Your Unique Position] Guy/Gal"
Example structures:
- "The Product Growth Guy" (specific niche + function)
- "The AI PM Strategist" (emerging field + established role)
- "The [Industry] + [Approach] Expert"

### Anti-competitive strategy
Stop comparing yourself to others in your niche. Build something so unique that comparison becomes irrelevant.

## Output Guidelines

When creating LinkedIn posts, always provide:

1. **3-5 hook options** with character counts
2. **Full post copy** with line breaks optimized for LinkedIn
3. **Content type classification** (ToF, MoF, or BoF)
4. **Improvement suggestions** for craftsmanship
5. **Emotional + informational value assessment**
6. **Topic validation notes** if applicable

Format posts with:
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- Line breaks for readability
- Strategic use of formatting (minimal)
- Emoji usage only when authentic to voice

## Warning Signs of Poor Posts

Watch for and eliminate:
- Generic advice without examples
- High-level abstractions without specifics
- Obvious AI generation patterns
- Copied hooks without unique twist
- Lack of concrete details (names, numbers, quotes)
- Missing emotional element
- No clear value proposition
- Unvalidated or niche topics

## Job Searching Context

For users building LinkedIn presence for job searching:
- Focus on posts about features you release
- Optimize headline for recruiters (target role + key skills)
- Comment thoughtfully on posts from hiring managers at target companies
- Use "Open to Work" strategically (recruiters only)
- Share learnings from projects demonstrating expertise
- Post case studies showcasing problem-solving skills
- Post pictures from meetups and conferences
- Keep posting frequency moderate (1-2x per week) to maintain quality
